About Quality of Service settings [待校准@11467]

Overview [待校准@11174]

ROS 2 offers a rich variety of Quality of Service (QoS) policies that allow you to tune communication between nodes. With the right set of Quality of Service policies, ROS 2 can be as reliable as TCP or as best-effort as UDP, with many, many possible states in between. Unlike ROS 1, which primarily only supported TCP, ROS 2 benefits from the flexibility of the underlying DDS transport in environments with lossy wireless networks where a “best effort” policy would be more suitable, or in real-time computing systems where the right Quality of Service profile is needed to meet deadlines. [待校准@11468]

A set of QoS “policies” combine to form a QoS “profile”. Given the complexity of choosing the correct QoS policies for a given scenario, ROS 2 provides a set of predefined QoS profiles for common use cases (e.g. sensor data). At the same time, developers are given the flexibility to control specific policies of the QoS profiles. [待校准@11469]

QoS profiles can be specified for publishers, subscriptions, service servers and clients. A QoS profile can be applied independently to each instance of the aforementioned entities, but if different profiles are used, it is possible that they will be incompatible, preventing the delivery of messages. [待校准@11470]

QoS policies [待校准@11471]

The base QoS profile currently includes settings for the following policies: [待校准@11472]

  • History [待校准@11473]

    • Keep last: only store up to N samples, configurable via the queue depth option. [待校准@11474]

    • Keep all: store all samples, subject to the configured resource limits of the underlying middleware. [待校准@11475]

  • Depth [待校准@11476]

    • Queue size: only honored if the “history” policy was set to “keep last”. [待校准@11477]

  • Reliability [待校准@11478]

    • Best effort: attempt to deliver samples, but may lose them if the network is not robust. [待校准@11479]

    • Reliable: guarantee that samples are delivered, may retry multiple times. [待校准@11480]

  • Durability [待校准@11481]

    • Transient local: the publisher becomes responsible for persisting samples for “late-joining” subscriptions. [待校准@11482]

    • Volatile: no attempt is made to persist samples. [待校准@11483]

  • Deadline [待校准@11484]

    • Duration: the expected maximum amount of time between subsequent messages being published to a topic [待校准@11485]

  • Lifespan [待校准@11486]

    • Duration: the maximum amount of time between the publishing and the reception of a message without the message being considered stale or expired (expired messages are silently dropped and are effectively never received). [待校准@11487]

  • Liveliness [待校准@11488]

    • Automatic: the system will consider all of the node’s publishers to be alive for another “lease duration” when any one of its publishers has published a message. [待校准@11489]

    • Manual by topic: the system will consider the publisher to be alive for another “lease duration” if it manually asserts that it is still alive (via a call to the publisher API). [待校准@11490]

  • Lease Duration [待校准@11491]

    • Duration: the maximum period of time a publisher has to indicate that it is alive before the system considers it to have lost liveliness (losing liveliness could be an indication of a failure). [待校准@11492]

For each of the policies that is not a duration, there is also the option of “system default”, which uses the default of the underlying middleware. For each of the policies that is a duration, there also exists a “default” option that means the duration is unspecified, which the underlying middleware will usually interpret as an infinitely long duration. [待校准@11493]

Comparison to ROS 1 [待校准@11494]

The “history” and “depth” policies in ROS 2 combine to provide functionality akin to the queue size in ROS 1. [待校准@11495]

The “reliability” policy in ROS 2 is akin to the use of either UDPROS (only in roscpp) for “best effort”, or TCPROS (ROS 1 default) for “reliable”. Note however that even the reliable policy in ROS 2 is implemented using UDP, which allows for multicasting if appropriate. [待校准@11496]

The “durability” policy “transient local”, combined with any depth, provides functionality similar to that of “latching” publishers. The remaining policies in ROS 2 are not akin to anything that is available in ROS 1, meaning that ROS 2 is more featureful than ROS 1 in this respect. It is possible that in the future, even more QoS policies will be available in ROS 2. [待校准@11497]

QoS profiles [待校准@11498]

Profiles allow developers to focus on their applications without worrying about every QoS setting possible. A QoS profile defines a set of policies that are expected to go well together for a particular use case. [待校准@11499]

The currently defined QoS profiles are: [待校准@11500]

  • Default QoS settings for publishers and subscriptions [待校准@11501]

    In order to make the transition from ROS 1 to ROS 2 easier, exercising a similar network behavior is desirable. By default, publishers and subscriptions in ROS 2 have “keep last” for history with a queue size of 10, “reliable” for reliability, “volatile” for durability, and “system default” for liveliness. Deadline, lifespan, and lease durations are also all set to “default”. [待校准@11502]

  • Services [待校准@11503]

    In the same vein as publishers and subscriptions, services are reliable. It is especially important for services to use volatile durability, as otherwise service servers that re-start may receive outdated requests. While the client is protected from receiving multiple responses, the server is not protected from side-effects of receiving the outdated requests. [待校准@11504]

  • Sensor data [待校准@11505]

    For sensor data, in most cases it’s more important to receive readings in a timely fashion, rather than ensuring that all of them arrive. That is, developers want the latest samples as soon as they are captured, at the expense of maybe losing some. For that reason the sensor data profile uses best effort reliability and a smaller queue size. [待校准@11506]

  • Parameters [待校准@11507]

    Parameters in ROS 2 are based on services, and as such have a similar profile. The difference is that parameters use a much larger queue depth so that requests do not get lost when, for example, the parameter client is unable to reach the parameter service server. [待校准@11508]

  • System default [待校准@11509]

    This uses the RMW implementation’s default values for all of the policies. Different RMW implementations may have different defaults. [待校准@11510]

Click here for the specific policies in use for the above profiles. The settings in these profiles are subject to further tweaks, based on the feedback from the community. [待校准@11511]

QoS compatibilities [待校准@11512]

Note: This section refers to publishers and subscriptions but the content applies to service servers and clients in the same manner. [待校准@11513]

QoS profiles may be configured for publishers and subscriptions independently. A connection between a publisher and a subscription is only made if the pair has compatible QoS profiles. [待校准@11514]

QoS profile compatibility is determined based on a “Request vs Offered” model. Subscriptions request a QoS profile that is the “minimum quality” that it is willing to accept, and publishers offer a QoS profile that is the “maximum quality” that it is able to provide. Connections are only made if every policy of the requested QoS profile is not more stringent than that of the offered QoS profile. Multiple subscriptions can be connected to a single publisher simultaneously even if their requested QoS profiles are different. The compatibility between a publisher and a subscription is unaffected by the presence of other publishers and subscriptions. [待校准@11515]

In order for a connection to be made, all of the policies that affect compatibility must be compatible. For example, even if a requested and offered QoS profile pair has compatible reliability QoS policies, but they have incompatible durability QoS policies, a connection will still not be made. [待校准@11538]

When connections are not made, no messages will be passed between the publisher and subscription. There are mechanisms to detect this situation, which will be covered in a later section. [待校准@11539]

Comparison to ROS 1 [待校准@11494]

Historically in ROS 1, any publisher and subscriber with the same message type on the same topic would be connected. The possibility of incompatible requested and offered QoS profiles is something new to be aware of when using ROS 2. [待校准@11540]

QoS events [待校准@11541]

Some QoS policies have possible events related to them. Developers may provide each publisher and subscription with callback functions that are triggered by these QoS events and handle them in a way they see fit, similar to how messages received on a topic are handled. [待校准@11542]

Developers may subscribe to the following QoS events that are associated with a publisher: [待校准@11543]

  • Offered deadline missed [待校准@11544]

    The publisher has not published a message within the expected duration that was set out by the deadline QoS policy. [待校准@11545]

  • Liveliness lost [待校准@11546]

    The publisher has failed to indicate its liveliness within the lease duration. [待校准@11547]

  • Offered incompatible QoS [待校准@11548]

    The publisher has encountered a subscription on the same topic that is requesting a QoS profile that the offered QoS profile cannot satisfy, resulting in no connection between the publisher and that subscription. [待校准@11549]

Developers may subscribe to the following QoS events that are associated with a subscription: [待校准@11550]

  • Requested deadline missed [待校准@11551]

    The subscription has not received a message within the expected duration that was set out by the deadline QoS policy. [待校准@11552]

  • Liveliness changed [待校准@11553]

    The subscription has noticed that one or more publishers on the subscribed topic has failed to indicate their liveliness within the lease duration. [待校准@11554]

  • Requested incompatible QoS [待校准@11555]

    The subscription has encountered a publisher on the same topic that is offering a QoS profile that does not satisfy the requested QoS profile, resulting in no connection between the subscription and that publisher. [待校准@11556]
